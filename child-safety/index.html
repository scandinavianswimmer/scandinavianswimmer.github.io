Child Safety Standards

DATEMAXXING is an 18+ dating app. We maintain a zero-tolerance policy against child sexual abuse and exploitation, child sexual abuse material, grooming, sextortion, trafficking, and any sexualized contact involving minors.

1. Published CSAE Standards

DATEMAXXING prohibits child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE), including any content or behavior that sexually exploits, abuses, or endangers a child. This includes child sexual abuse material (CSAM), grooming, sextortion, sexual trafficking, soliciting sexual content from a minor, sexualized roleplay involving minors, and attempts to move a minor to another platform for sexual contact.

2. Age Gate and Verification

  • Users must be 18 or older.
  • Date of birth is required during onboarding.
  • Accounts suspected of being underage may be suspended or deleted.
  • Live capture and vendor verification may be used to support age and identity checks.

3. Reporting and User Feedback

Users can report underage users, exploitation, harassment, impersonation, scams, and other safety concerns from inside the app. Reports involving minors or exploitation are prioritized as urgent.

Child-safety contact: Luke Mladenoff — luke.mladenoff@gmail.com

4. CSAM Handling

When we obtain actual knowledge of CSAM or CSAE activity, we take action according to our policies and applicable law. Actions may include removing content, preserving evidence where legally appropriate, suspending or banning accounts, preventing further contact, and making required reports to lawful authorities or child-safety organizations.

5. Enforcement

  • Immediate account suspension or ban for CSAE/CSAM activity.
  • Urgent internal review routing for reports involving minors.
  • Fraud and abuse flags for accounts associated with exploitation reports.
  • Cooperation with lawful requests and required child-safety reporting obligations.

6. Store and Legal Compliance

These standards are designed to support app-store child-safety requirements for dating/social apps, including public CSAE standards, in-app feedback, CSAM response, legal compliance, and a child-safety point of contact.
